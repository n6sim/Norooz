so there's a new year happening this Friday, March 20th.
not a calendar someone made up in a meeting room.
the actual moment the universe flips the switch.

the spring equinox. day and night, perfectly equal.
nature's reset button.

humans noticed this 3,000 years ago and said —
yeah, this is it. this is the new year.

and honestly? they were right.

the moment

when exactly tho 📅

10:45:59 AM Friday, March 20th 2026 · Eastern Time

not "around spring." not "sometime in March."
the exact second the sun crosses the equator.
Iranians have been tracking this moment for thousands of years.
your calendar app could never.

the ritual

okay but what do we actually do 🌿

first we clean everything. like everything.
we call it khane tekani.
shake the dust of the whole year out.
new year, clean slate. literally.

then we put on new clothes.
because you're not bringing last year's energy
into this one.

then we set the table. 🌿
and that table? it's a whole thing.

the table

the Haft-Seen table ✨

seven items. all starting with S in Persian.
not decoration. intention. every item is a prayer without words.

the people

then we show up for each other 💚

we visit family. starting with the elders.
we reconcile. had beef with someone this year?
Nowruz is when you fix it.
no grudges allowed into the new year.

and we give Eydi.
gifts, usually money, from elders to the young.
a blessing passed hand to hand.
basically the Persian version of
"here, start fresh."

it's not just a holiday.
it's a reminder that the people around you
are the whole point.

the legend

the guy who made it all make sense 🌹

"the renewal of the world"
— Omar Khayyam, describing Nowruz, 11th century

meet Omar Khayyam.
11th century Persian poet, mathematician, astronomer.
yes, all three. the original multi-hyphenate.

he reformed the Iranian calendar to lock Nowruz
to the exact moment of the equinox.
so accurate it helped shape the Gregorian calendar
you use today.

same guy wrote poetry about wine, roses,
and the meaning of life.
a whole legend.

the celebration

13 days. then nature. 🌍

Nowruz is not one day. it's 13 days of celebration.
visiting, eating, laughing, just existing together.

on the 13th day, Sizdah Bedar,
nobody stays inside.
everyone goes out. parks, rivers, fields, wherever.
spend the whole day in nature.
breathe it in. let go of whatever you're still carrying.

Iranians built a national holiday
around going outside and being grateful.
3,000 years ago.

we been knew. 🌿

the culture

what Persian culture is actually about 🔥

people think they know Iran.
they've seen the news.

but here's what doesn't make the headlines:

a civilization that built its whole ethics
around three words.
from Zoroastrianism, thousands of years
before anything you learned in school:

پندار نیک  ·  گفتار نیک  ·  کردار نیک

good thoughts. good words. good deeds.
that's literally it.

the mullahs tried to erase it.
it didn't work. it never has.
Nowruz survived every empire, every conquest, every ban.
this year, as Iranians fight for their freedom —
it means everything.
